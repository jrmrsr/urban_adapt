Stormwater and Flooding

PROTECT YOUR PROPERTY. PROTECT YOUR COMMUNITY. PLUG THE GAP.

Hundreds of millions of dollars are lost every year in Ontario due to flood-related events. Properties can be equipped to reduce the risk of water damage within their boundaries and their community at large. That’s not the hard part. The hard part is finding the resources to build that protection.

Urban Adapt is a collection of practical summaries, guidelines and opportunities to reduce cost of implementation. It was created to empower property owners’ action in order to create resilient and sustainable communities.

What is stormwater? Why does it matter?

Stormwater is a term used to refer to rain and/or melted snow once it reaches the ground. Some stormwater will be absorbed into the ground through a process called infiltration. The remaining stormwater, referred to as runoff, runs along the surface of the ground until it is collected. When the stormwater accumulates above the surface, it causes flooding.

One of the root causes of urban flooding is the increased area of impermeable surfaces such as pavement and rooftops that prevent water from infiltrating into the soil.

Flooding can also be caused by ice jams and rapid snowmelt and the risk of flooding is increased alongside the risk of extreme weather.

Canada’s flood damage costs were approximately US$2.4 billion in 2010, this number is expected to triple by 2030 if no efforts are made to mitigate the risks.

It’s a big price tag but that’s only if we can’t mitigate the risks. By implementing property-level mitigation strategies, property owners and residents have the opportunity to ‘plug the gap’ in the public flood protection schemes. Because protecting your property also protects the community, there are many tax-rebates, grants and bursaries that exist to help with the upfront costs of property-level flood prevention and control.
