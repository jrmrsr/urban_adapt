Stormwater Reduction Measures

The features on your property are designed to allow rainwater to absorb into the ground slowly and filter pollutants. These features include:

  • Rain Barrel
  • Rain Garden
  • Sump
  • Absorbent Landscaping
  • Rain-Friendly Driveways & Paving
  • Detention Tanks

Not all features are used on all properties. The features used for your property may vary based on the amount of paved area, slope, and whether water (runoff) from your home leads to a stream.

Rain barrels are a form of above ground water storage. The rain barrels capture the rain from the roof of a house. This allows the water collected to be used in the future, or to be slowly released and therefore allowing the water to infiltrate through the ground.

Rain gardens are gardens, however they are usually at a sunken level, allowing the water to collect and slowly infiltrate into the ground. The plants in the rain garden are usually special plants that thrive in both dry and wet conditions.

A sump is a hole where the storm water is collected. The water entering the sump will be filtered and then make its way to the groundwater.

Layering the landscaping allows water to filter through the top layer of the soil faster, and avoid flooding or draining into the local sewer system.

Rain-friendly paving involves having spaces for the rainwater to infiltrate through, rather than turn into runoff and enter the stormwater system. The rain-friendly paving is usually sloped to allow the water to drain onto the grass or between the paving into the ground, as opposed to the street.

A detention tank is a tank that temporarily stores the water from the rain and slowly releases it. These tanks can be both above ground or underground, however they are more commonly found underground and connected to the sump.
