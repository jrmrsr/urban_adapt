Financial Incentives

From a financial perspective, why does it make sense to protect your home from stormwater flooding?

Floods are now the most expensive type of natural disaster in Canada. In recent years, the frequency and severity of stormwater events have increased across the country. Climate change makes these events more likely and land use change associated with urbanization worsens the consequences [source].

The costs associated with cleaning, decontamination, repairs, temporary relocation, and replacement of personal property typically run in the thousands to tens-of-thousands of dollars. The average cost of basement flood damage in an urban area is $42,000 [source].

The problem is that the average home insurance does not cover this type of damage, leaving property owners to pay massive unexpected bills. By considering proactive measures to protect your property, you reduce the risk of this damage significantly. Visit our Protect Your Property page for information.

Are there government incentives for property owners?

Depending on where your property is located, there are financial incentives for property owners to invest in stormwater protection measures.

  • Receive a refund on your stormwater charge based on measures implemented on your property
  • Eligible measures
    • Peak flow reduction
    • Water quality treatment
    • Runoff vlume reduction
    • Pollution prevention
Learn more at the City of Mississauga website

  • Receive a refund on your stormwater charge based on measures implemented on your property
  • Eligible measures
    • Rain barrels
    • Trees
    • Infiltration basins
    • Other methods for storing runoff
Learn more at the City of Waterloo website

  • For business properties, you may receive a refund on your stormwater charge based on measures implemented on your property including
    • Peak flow reduction
    • Water quality treatment
    • Runoff volume reduction
    • Operations and activities
  • The City of Guelph sells discounted rain barrels to residents
  • The City of Guelph offers a rebate up to $2,000 for residents who install a rain garden on their property
Learn more at the City of Guelph website
